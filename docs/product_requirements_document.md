## **Example Scenario: E-commerce Wishlist Feature**

Let's imagine we're building a new "Wishlist" feature for an existing e-commerce platform.

---

**Project Title:** E-commerce Wishlist Feature v1.0

**Document Version:** 1.0
**Date:** 2023-10-27
**Author(s):** Alex Product (Product Manager)
**Status:** Approved

---

### **1. Executive Summary**

*   **Problem/Opportunity:** Users often browse products they like but aren't ready to purchase immediately, leading to missed sales opportunities and poor user experience when trying to recall items later. A wishlist provides a solution for saving desired items.
*   **Solution:** Implement a user-friendly "Add to Wishlist" feature across product pages and a dedicated "My Wishlist" section for logged-in users.
*   **Impact:** Increase user engagement, improve conversion rates for items added to wishlists, and provide valuable data on user preferences.
*   **Goal:** Enhance the shopping experience and drive future purchases by allowing users to save and track desired products.

---

### **2. Problem Statement / Opportunity**

*   **Current State:** Our current e-commerce platform lacks a personal saving mechanism for products. Users either add items to their cart (which might inflate cart abandonment rates) or rely on external notes/memory, leading to frustration and lost potential sales.
*   **Why now?** Competitor analysis shows that similar platforms offer wishlists, indicating a standard feature users expect. Internal data suggests a high bounce rate from product pages where users show interest but don't convert immediately. This feature addresses a clear user need and a market gap.
*   **Evidence/Data:** User surveys (Q3 2023) showed 45% of respondents desired a way to save products for later. Cart abandonment rate is at 70%, some of which are likely users saving items temporarily.

---

### **3. Goals & Objectives**

*   **Overall Project Goal:** Improve user engagement and long-term conversion on the platform.
*   **SMART Objectives:**
    *   Increase the number of unique users interacting with the wishlist feature by 20% within 2 months post-launch.
    *   Achieve a 5% conversion rate from "Wishlist" to "Purchase" for items saved for over 24 hours, within 3 months post-launch.
    *   Reduce product page bounce rate by 3% for logged-in users who interact with the wishlist within 1 month post-launch.

---

### **4. Target Audience / Users**

*   **Primary Users:** Existing logged-in customers who browse products but don't always make an immediate purchase.
*   **User Personas:** "Savvy Shopper Sarah" (likes to compare and plan purchases), "Gift Giver Gary" (needs to remember items for others), "Window Shopper Wendy" (browses for inspiration).
*   **Secondary Users:** Our marketing team (for understanding product interest) and customer support (for assisting users with saved items).

---

### **5. Scope Definition (In & Out)**

*   **In Scope (MVP):**
    *   Logged-in users can "Add to Wishlist" from Product Detail Pages (PDPs) and Product Listing Pages (PLPs).
    *   Logged-in users can view their personalized "My Wishlist" page.
    *   Users can remove items from their wishlist.
    *   Wishlist items display basic product info (image, name, price, availability).
    *   Items in the wishlist will reflect current pricing and stock status.
    *   Ability to add items from wishlist directly to cart.
*   **Out of Scope (for v1.0):**
    *   Public or private wishlist sharing functionality.
    *   Price drop notifications for wishlist items.
    *   Organizing wishlists into multiple lists/categories.
    *   Wishlist collaboration or group wishlists.
    *   "Move to another list" functionality.

---

### **6. Key Features / Functionality (High-Level)**

*   **Add to Wishlist Button:** A clearly visible button/icon on PDPs and PLPs for adding an item.
*   **My Wishlist Page:** A dedicated section in the user's account dashboard displaying all saved items.
*   **Wishlist Item Management:** Functionality to view item details, check current stock/price, and remove items.
*   **Add to Cart from Wishlist:** A direct button to move an item from the wishlist to the shopping cart.
*   **Empty State:** A user-friendly message and call-to-action when the wishlist is empty.

---

### **7. Success Metrics & KPIs**

*   **Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):**
    *   Percentage of logged-in users who add at least one item to a wishlist per month.
    *   Average number of items per wishlist.
    *   Wishlist-to-Cart conversion rate.
    *   Wishlist-to-Purchase conversion rate.
    *   Customer Support tickets related to "saving items" (expect a decrease).

---

### **8. Technical Considerations / Requirements (Brief)**

*   **Database:** Requires a new database table or schema modification to store user-to-product wishlist associations.
*   **API:** New API endpoints for adding, removing, and retrieving wishlist items.
*   **Frontend Integration:** UI components for the "Add to Wishlist" button/icon and the "My Wishlist" page.
*   **User Authentication:** Feature must be tied to a logged-in user account.
*   **Scalability:** Solution must be able to handle millions of wishlist items and concurrent requests.

---

### **9. Dependencies & Risks**

*   **Dependencies:**
    *   Login/Authentication system stability.
    *   Product information management (PIM) system for accurate product details.
    *   Design team availability for UI/UX mocks and flows.
*   **Risks:**
    *   **Risk:** Low user adoption if the feature isn't discoverable or intuitive.
        *   **Mitigation:** Extensive A/B testing on button placement and wording; in-app onboarding tour.
    *   **Risk:** Performance degradation if wishlist queries are inefficient.
        *   **Mitigation:** Early performance testing, database indexing, caching strategies.
    *   **Risk:** Scope creep from stakeholders requesting advanced features in v1.0.
        *   **Mitigation:** Clear communication of "out of scope" items and a strong product backlog for future iterations.

*   **Phase 3 (Development/QA - MVP):** Weeks 4-9 (Backend development, Frontend integration, Unit/Integration/UAT testing)
*   **Phase 4 (Launch):** End of Week 10 (Controlled rollout, A/B testing)

*   **Next Steps:**
